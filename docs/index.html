About school

Concept

The EMS-SCS Summer School 2021 is dedicated to experimental methods in social and cognitive sciences.

Program

The School will consist of N courses including both theoretical lectures and practical workshops. The working language is English.

Location and format

The summer school will be held in a mixed format. Considering traveling limitations as well as health risks during global pandemic, we expect most of the participants and lecturers to join us online. However, applicants who will have an opportunity and a desire to participate offline, are welcome to join us at the Higher School of Economics in Saint Petersburg. We will ensure that both online and offline participants will have equal involvement in the discussion.

Organizer

Laboratory for Social and Cognitive Informatics (SCILA) at the National Research University Higher School of Economics (HSE) in Saint Petersburg, Russia.

Traveling

Unfortunately, due to the global pandemic of COVID-19, we cannot expect all of the participants to attend the School offline given the existing traveling restrictions. However, if you are able and willing to come, we will be pleased to welcome you at the Higher School of Economics in Saint Petersburg.

Although we strongly hope that we will have more traveling opportunities by the time of our school, we cannot guarantee international participants a possibility to issue a visa on a short notice. Thus, if you need a visa to enter Russia but it might be challenging to get in on time, we advise you to participate online in order to avoid potential difficulties. We hope for your understanding and will answer any further questions regarding the possibility of your visit.

Organizers

The EMS-SCS Summer School 2021 is organized by Laboratory for Social and Cognitive Informatics (SCILA) which is based at the National Research University Higher School of Economics (HSE) in Saint Petersburg, Russia. SCILA is an interdisciplinary team of researchers that studies human interactions with information, technologies, and other humans relying on social, behavioral, cognitive, and computer sciences.
